NeoGenesis

“We know the world by what we see: through a window, in a frame, on a screen.” — Anne Friedberg

My concept for this project was developed under the umbrella of Simulacra as described by Jean Baudrillard, exploring the relationship between the human body and computerised technologies.

In a technologically attuned civilisation, we are surrounded by systems that augment, extend, and subtly reshape the human body.

Drawing on Donna Haraway’s concept of “The Cyborg”, the work reflects on how embodiment, subjectivity, and experience are mediated through technological systems.

I approached this project as an attempt to visualise the symbiotic relationship between humans and technology — particularly “Cyborg Intentionality” as described by Don Ihde.

As realities become increasingly simulated, the boundary between real and virtual grows porous, altering how we understand ourselves and each other.
